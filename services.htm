PAP Smear

Colposcopy

Colposcopy is a diagnostic procedure used to closely examine the cervix, vagina, and vulva for signs of disease, especially after abnormal results from a Pap smear or HPV test.

LEEP

Loop Electrosurgical Excision Procedure (LEEP) is a treatment used to remove abnormal or precancerous cells from the cervix, typically following an abnormal Pap smear or colposcopy.

Myomectomy

A myomectomy is a surgical procedure to remove uterine fibroids, which are noncancerous growths of the uterus. Unlike a hysterectomy, a myomectomy aims to preserve the uterus, making it a popular option for women who wish to maintain their fertility or keep their uterus intact.

TAH - Advanced Cancer Operations

Total Abdominal Hysterectomy (TAH) is a surgical procedure in which the uterus is completely removed through an abdominal incision. In advanced cancer operations, TAH may be performed to treat or manage cancers of the reproductive system, including advanced-stage cervical, uterine, or ovarian cancer.
